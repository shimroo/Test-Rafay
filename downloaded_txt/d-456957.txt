new york
cdf-ny has been working on children’s advocacy since 1992.  today, we collaborate with children, youth and families to advance collective action around youth and family justice, economic mobility and health equity.

dignity in schools
join the dignity in schools campaign-new york, part of a nationwide effort to dismantle the school-to-prison pipeline. we mobilize parents, youth, advocates, and educators in new york, aiming to revolutionize our local schools. cdf-new york, in a multi-party coalition, works tirelessly to change nyc’s punitive school discipline policies, fostering an environment of dignity, respect, and quality education. stand with us for a safe, healthy, and inclusive school system that empowers every student to thrive. 

        learn more

        


 






end child poverty
explore end child poverty now-new york, a campaign addressing the child poverty crisis. our 2019 report advocates investing 1.4% more of the federal budget into existing programs, potentially reducing child poverty by 57% nationally and 58% in new york. join us to help lift 5.5 million children and youth out of poverty. 

        learn more

        


 






raise the age
join raise the age-new york, a movement championing the implementation of a law raising the age of criminal responsibility in the state. this law is a product of collaborative efforts from diverse individuals and organizations. we celebrate this milestone and continue our advocacy for comprehensive reform in juvenile justice. 

        learn more

        


 






solutions not suspensions
students, especially black, latinx, and students with disabilities, are disproportionately affected by suspensions, causing them to lose vital classroom time. cdf-ny stands with the solutions not suspension coalition, working to enhance school environments, curb the school-to-prison pipeline, and ensure every student feels respected and supported.

        learn more

        


 






whole child, whole community
join youth advocates, community leaders, and state officials as we outline the urgent budget requirements for children, youth, and families. new york must prioritize these investments—now. be a part of this community-driven vision by taking action. 

        learn more

youthnpower
in collaboration with the cuny graduate center’s public science project, the center for the study of social policy, and new yorkers for children, youthnpower: transforming care is a collective that includes young people age 18 to 25 with lived expertise in the child welfare system and coming from over-surveilled communities, working with researchers, policy advocates and organizers over 2 years to explore, document and uplift the power, needs, and desires of young people transitioning out of foster care as young adults in new york city.
