cobs,
        called macuquinas in spanish,  are a crude style of hand hammered
        coins, struck in spain and spanish america.  this site is for those
        cobs that were made in the spanish territory of what is now colombia. 
        these coins are typically crudely made irregular shaped pieces, it is
        this style that makes them intriguing, along with the fact that they are
        lore from the spanish conquest of america.  as i hold these coins
        and study them, my mind envisions the explorations and discoveries of
        the conquistadors and of pirates searching the seas for ships carrying
        these "pieces of eight" back to spain.  for a fuller,
        more eloquent description please see a copy of the general cob book, the
        practical book of cobs by daniel
        and frank sedwick.  for the best books specializing in colombian
        cobs,  in spanish, get a copy of  the macuquinas de colombia (the cobs of colombia,
        south america) by restrepo, dr. jorge emilio & lasser,
        joseph r. or in english get the second edition the
        cob coinage of colombia by joseph r. lasser and jorge emilio
        restrepo.  throughout this web site the spanish version is referred
        to as r/l and the english version is referred to as l/r.  see reference page for publication data.
 
this site
        is devoted to presenting a reference collection for educational
        purposes. the focus is on colombian cobs but not exclusively, i do touch on other related
        subjects.  i have been collecting since 1960 and have often found
        it difficult to find sources of reliable information.  even though
        information may exist, the problem remains to identify it, then after
        identifying it, one has to locate it.  with the advent of the world
        wide web information is easier to find but the content is often
        inadequate.  hopefully this site will add content to the world wide
        web.  this site is my contribution to the numismatic community and
        to all other interested persons, by my presentation here i hope to
        further knowledge and interest in colombian coinage, the culture and
        people of colombia.  this is not a buy/sell market place, however
        if you have coins to sell i would appreciate an email offering such. i
        hope you enjoy it, and please credit the author for any use of materials
        in this site, and respect the copyrights of others whose works are
        referred to here.

to quote burzio.........
        "the peninsular mints, seville, burgos, granada, segovia, madrid, etc., used the
        same coining process that was exported to the indies. it did not conform to
        regulations  in regards to shape and uniformity of dies.  in spite of official
        orders, the coins were made using a very defective and primitive coining
        technique; hand hammering, that shows  imperfection in figures and
        legend. poorly stamped  and incomplete coins resulted from using shallow dies and 
        irregular punches." *
