you need lights
many people are unaware that vermont bicycle law was changed 
                    a few years ago to better reflect a cyclist's status as a 
                    vehicle. front and rear lights are now required on 
                    all bikes from 1/2-hour after sunset until 1/2-hour before 
                    sunrise. operating a vehicle without proper lighting is a 
                    moving violation and carries a pretty stiff fine (like 80 
                    bucks or so). we have lots of lighting options for less than 
                    $80.
the light & motion vis500 is a good example of a modern, 
                    usb-rechargeable light. for $52.95 you get 500 lumens of led 
                    lighting powered up by a li-ion battery you recharge from 
                    a micro usb port. high beam, low beam, flashing modes, and 
                    the whole thing weighs about the same as the pocketful ofquarters 
                    you might have carried to shovel into a brattleboro parking 
                    meter.
good lights are one thing you can't afford to be without, 
                    especially at this time of year!

vermont e-bike incentives for 2025





the state of vermont, as part of the pressing effort to reduce 
                    fossil fuel usage in transportation, has refined and renewed 
                    its incentive program to help people get out of old cars and 
                    onto e-bikes. the program, created for the summer of 2022, 
                    ran through the money allocated for it in just a couple of 
                    months and lots of lessons were learned. the program was tightened 
                    up, streamlined, and given increased funding for 2024. there 
                    are political headwinds in the way of funding for 2025 and 
                    a fight is on. watch this space!

awards are income-sensitive
it matters that you will use this to reduce car use—not 
                      just for recreation
up to $800 for cargo bikes and $400 for regular, single 
                      bikes
these awards are in addition to the $200 grants available 
                      from green mountain power.

read up on the program and apply online at 
https://www.driveelectricvt.com/incentives/vermont-state-incentives#ebikes
