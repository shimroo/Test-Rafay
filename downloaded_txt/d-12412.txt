image/b/d/bdb8e69345c08d0fffe3217f4322c5e4_1.png languageenglish preview samplethe penn state agronomy guide   description   the penn state agronomy guide is designed as a quick reference for information on crop and soil management, including varieties, nutritional requirements, establishment, and harvesting; storing seed and grain; and farm management and budgeting. recommendations for managing weed, insect, and disease pests in corn, grain sorghum, soybean, small grains, and forages are provided in part 2.
this comprehensive guide is revised biennially by  penn state extension specialists in agronomy, entomology, and plant pathology; u.s. pasture laboratory staff; county extension educators; seed, fertilizer, lime, and pesticide industry personnel; and representatives of state agricultural and environmental government agencies.
the current version was released in january 2025.  who is this for?farm managers, producers, and growers on farms of any size what will you learn?crop management (corn, grain sorghum, small grains, forages, cover crops), soil management (soil fertility management, nontraditional soil amendments), organic crop production basics, enterprise budgeting, and pest management for corn, sorghum, soybean, small grains, and forages fees  agenda  instructors  reviews  write your own review only registered users can write reviews. please sign in or create an account  faqs are all publications available in both digital and print versions?
