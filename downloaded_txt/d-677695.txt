            update: wind advisory begins tonight; much colder temperatures to follow

updated 
feb 16, 2025



batavia — a wind advisory has been issued for western new york.the advisory will be in effect 7 p.m. sunday to 6 p.m. monday, according to the national weather service in buffalo. winds of 25 to 25 mph are expected with gusts up to 50 mph.gusty winds will blow around unsecured objects, according to the nws. tree limbs could be blown down and a few power outages may result.the wind advisory includes all of western new york, including genesee, livingston, orleans and wyoming counties.in the meantime, a winter weather advisory remains in effect through 1 a.m. monday for all of western new york.mixed precipitation is expected. total snow accumulations of 4 to 7 inches may occur, along with ice accumulations about 3/10 of an inch.winds will also gust as high as 45 mph and isolated power outages may occur, according to the nws.periods of snow, sleet or freezing rain will result in slippery roads and limited visibilities, according to the nws. drivers are advised to plan on slippery road conditions.
