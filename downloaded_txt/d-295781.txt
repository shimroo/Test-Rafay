cool thing of the week





february 10th, 2025
	  i was just sorting my box of 1985's and i noticed something i never noticed before.  there are two distinctly different types of backs.  one is matte and the other is glossy.  they are very easy to tell apart, it's not like there are ambiguous ones in between.  clearly these were printed in two batches.  i'm going to have to start building sets of each type.  as far as i can tell neither is less common.  you learn something every day in wacky pack land.

ebay versus the wacky mall


oh boy this is a bad one.  $4,900 for a 16th series set?  plus tax that well over $5k.
      
this set sold for $2400 on the wacky mall.  there's another comparable set still available on the wacky mall for $2,400.  or for just another $800 they could have gotten a complete 1-16 with all variations.
      
how can somebody spend that kind of money without doing the slightest bit of research?  two minutes of effort and they could have saved $3,000.

 



speaking of not doing research, this person made just as big of a mistake as a seller.  how hard is it to look up other listings of the items?  if you have no idea what something is worth, doesn't it make sense to run an auction rather than list it buy-it-now for $17.12?

roxanne and jay lynch, 1992
  
december 13th, 2024.
    it is with heavy heart that we announce the passing of roxanne toser. roxanne was without a doubt the singular pioneer in collcting wacky packs.
    
 roxanne was doing this in the 1980's and 1990's when nobody else was.  she wrote the first book on wackys and also the second one.  she started and ran the philadelphia non-sports card show for decades - the only place people still meet face-to-face in the modern world.  and she did all this without having been seven years old in 1973.  she just recognized greatness when she saw it and she is responsible for making this hobby what it is.  roxanne will be sorely missed.  rest in peace roxanne, the hobby will never forget you.
