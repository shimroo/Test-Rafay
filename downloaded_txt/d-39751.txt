the beets – “time brought age” (stereogum premiere)





the beets, the jackson heights (via uruguay) project of juan wauters,  seem to show-up on more brooklyn diy bills than any other band i can  think of … fitting for a group that remind me an older, grittier, less  guarded nyc. i’ve seen folks reference the ramones (maybe because  they’re from queens?), but i’m more reminded of shimmy disc via uncle  wiggly, fly ashtray, etc. and the warping, quietly imploding  ’60s-referencing pop stuff  that might show up on a vintage chemical imbalance  compilation (thinking fellers on their best behavior, et al). woods’  jarvis taveniere recorded these two new tracks for the “time brought  age” 7″.  crystal stilts’ jb townsend mixed. the laconic a-side features  12-string guitar, keyboards, tambourine, recorder, and a hazy urban  pastoral feel. (they re-enter the garage for the 2-minute b-side.)  someone makes a reference to the stones’ between the buttons in  the release’s press materials: why not sync with the band’s humor (see  jagger-nodding cover), go for some version of the bad beetween the buttons pun, and call it a day? listen: the beets – “time brought age” download
