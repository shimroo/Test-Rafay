  ocala florida
history brief:
ocala is the seat of marion county florida and
was incorporated
in 1868. ocala started out as a
trade and processing center for citrus fruit, vegetables, and other
farm goods. the ocala area is known for its
thoroughbred horses, cattle, lumber, and phosphates. ocala florida is
commonly referred to as "the horse capital of the world". tourism is
also important to ocala while fish and game abound in the many
nearby lakes and streams and in the ocala national forest. silver
springs, a nature theme park featuring glass-bottomed boats, is
adjacent to the city. the site was named for the nearby native american
village "ocali" (visited by de soto in 1539).
