            carbon capture and utilization
      
 
conventional carbon capture technologies have been unable to economically address the co2 emissions generated by large, stationary point sources. our team is exploring co2 capture and utilization technologies that could dramatically impact the fossil fuel–based power production and industrial sectors.

wildfires
seung-hyun cho, phd, a leading researcher on air quality at rti international, is available for interviews to discuss the health impacts of air pollution and strategies to mitigate its effects, particularly in the context of wildfires and extreme weather events. 
learn more  about wildfires


energy
sameer parvathikar, phd, rti's director of renewable energy and energy storage, is available for interviews on e-methanol production and other cutting-edge energy technologies.
learn more  about energy


water resources
michael kane, director of rti's center for water resources, develops real-time river forecasting and decision support for water resources systems. his expertise helps water planners and emergency managers understand the risks of extreme weather events. 
learn more  about water resources


plastics
imari walker-franklin, phd, is a research chemist and microplastics expert. she can speak to the connection between microplastics and pfas and how chemicals accumulate in people's bodies and cause harm.
