selden rodman left us last saturday, november 2, 2002 after a long and richly productive life,
                        characterized by individuality and non-conformity. he was 93 years old.
                        if there was a haitian art hall of fame, he would have to be a charter member.

after his first visit to haiti in 1938, he wrote a play on the life of toussaint l'ouverture
                          which opened in port-au-prince in 1942. 


he served in the oss during wwii and then returned to haiti
                    where he was co-director of the centre d'art and
                    was among those who supervised the landmark mural project at trinity cathedral. 
selden wrote more than 40 books, poetry, prose and
                    of course,his authoritive works on haitian and brazilian art.
                    his classic, "haiti, the black republic" published in 1954,
                    is still an insightful guide to the haitian culture and spirit
                    even though much of the tourist information is outdated after half a century.
besides his books, he left us a wonderful collection of folkart, haitian, mexican, cuban and more,
                    at ramapo college in northern new jersey. 
our sympathies to his wife of 40 years, carole, and his children, bebe, carla and van.
                    we will miss him too.

december 2, 2000 gwen grant mellon will be buried today
                    on a hillside overlooking the albert schweitzer hospital in deschapelles, haiti,
                    the hospital that she and her late husband, dr. larry mellon, founded in 1954.
                    mrs. mellon passed away this past wednesday in miami
                    due to complications during hip surgery.
                    she visited her family in pittsburgh often over the years and
                    i felt priveleged to know her.
                    she was a great friend to haiti and to haitian art as well,;
                    she encouraged and supported saincilus ismael
                    and the many students of his who painted in and around deschapelles .

godspeed to mrs. mellon.

two of the brightest stars in the haitian art firmament flickered out in february, 2000
                    as saincilus ismael and pierre -joseph valcin
                    "returned to guinee" as they say in haiti when someone passes on.
                    ismael, the master of deschappelles, inspired an entire school of artists
                    in the artibonite with his depictions of haitian life rendered in byzantine detail.
                    p-j valcin, the " prince of the naives" was one of the precious
                    few self-taught artists still painting.
