a cozy refuge in the wilderness of banff national park














nestled in the company of towering peaks and montane meadows of banff national park, shadow lake lodge is a 14km  hike, ski, or snowshoe from the trailhead.  enjoy private cabins and shared cozy communal spaces for your summer or winter getaway; an unforgettable backcountry experience.






















private spaces

unwind at the end of the day in your own private cabin with friends or loved ones. 











breathtaking scenery 

surround yourself in the beautiful landscapes of banff national park.











a place to connect

form lasting memories with fellow visitors at shadow lake lodge.











elevated backcountry facilities

heated washrooms, cabins, and hot running water. a treat for backcountry accommodation.

enjoy summer in the alpine.



hike to your heart’s content in banff’s backyard. visitors can follow an excellent system of hiking trails to unspoiled bodies of water, along glacial valleys, and to alpine passes where crowds are scarce and human impact has been minimal.


























experience winter wonderland.



there is nothing quite like waking up to the hushed silence of snow falling. whether you snowshoe, cross-country ski, or backcountry tour, shadow lake lodge offers options for all enthusiasts.

shadow lake lodge and the alpine club of canada are grateful for the opportunity we have to work and play on the land known today as canada. we recognize that these lands are home to the enduring presence of all first nations and métis people, and the inuit.shadow lake lodge is located on the traditional lands of treaty 7, comprised of the stoney nakoda nations of wesley, chiniki, and bearspaw; three nations of the blackfoot confederacy: the piikani, kainai, and siksika; the mountain cree; and the tsuu t'ina of the dene people. this territory is also shared with the métis nation of alberta, region iii. before the signing of treaty 7, and prior to the establishment of provincial boundaries, this region was also used by the ktunaxa and the maskwacis people. we acknowledge the past, present, and future generations of these nations who lead us in stewarding this land, as well as honour their knowledge and cultural ties to this place.
