what are lemurs?
lemurs are a type of primate that is native to madagascar, an island off the coast of east africa. they are known for their large eyes and long tails, which they use to balance themselves while jumping from tree to tree.
there are over 100 species of lemurs, ranging in size from the 30-gram (1 ounce) pygmy mouse lemur to the 9-kilogram (20-pound) indri. they come in a variety of colors and patterns, including white, black, brown, and gray.




lemur conservation efforts
unfortunately, many lemur species are endangered due to habitat destruction, hunting, and climate change. as a result, conservation efforts are crucial to protect these unique animals.
