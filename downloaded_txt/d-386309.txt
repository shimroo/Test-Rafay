city offices to be closed feb. 17 for presidents' day 


geneva's city offices will be closed for business feb. 17 in observance of presidents' day. garbage pickup will remain on the regular schedule.						read on...







police investigating multiple car burglaries 


the geneva police department is asking residents to lock their car doors after nearly 20 vehicles were reported burglarized early feb. 13.						read on...







geneva police officers to take the polar plunge 


the geneva police department will be taking the polar plunge sunday, march 2 in yorkville to raise money for special olympics illinois athletes.						read on...







comed to trim trees near electric lines in february 


comed will be trimming trees to improve electric reliability in several locations in february, predominantly east of the fox river in geneva.						read on...







city removes referendum initiative from ballot 


the city will not be proceeding with a referendum question to improve its public safety facilities after the city council voted jan. 22 to rescind the measure.						read on...







raise a glass for trees at geneva fundraiser 


a night of fine wine paired with appetizers and live auction sounds like a perfect night out and supports a worthy cause of planting more trees in geneva.						read on...







ride in kane fare increase starts in february 


ride in kane will implement a fare increase starting in february to continue delivering services to senior citizens and disabled residents.						read on...







geneva police officers receive lifesaving awards 


geneva police chief eric passarelli presented life saving medals to sgt. mark russo, officers dan yates, bob pech, and sarah sullivan on jan. 6.						read on...
