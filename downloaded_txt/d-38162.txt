reaching for the stars 
reaching for the stars at triton leaders conference
uc san diego has solidified its position as a global leader in sustainability by earning a top rating in the sustainability tracking assessment and rating system (stars). ranking third in the nation among platinum-rated institutions, we lead the way in sustainable innovation, reducing emissions, advancing climate education and empowering our community through groundbreaking initiatives such as the jane teranes climate change education requirement for incoming first-year students and the blue line trolley extension. learn about the rating 











beyond a traditional transcript	        uc san diego’s co-curricular record (ccr) is redefining how students showcase their experiences beyond the classroom. offered through the teaching and learning commons, this official university document verifies student achievements in everything from research and internships to mentorship and global engagement—providing a powerful complement to academic transcripts. unlike a self-reported resume, the ccr undergoes a rigorous validation process, ensuring employers and graduate programs recognize the depth of a student’s skills and contributions.

arts at uc san diego
from broadway-bound plays and captivating musical performances by grammy-winning artists to futuristic film screenings and gravity-defying sculptures, uc san diego’s vibrant art scene has something for everyone. there are hundreds of visual and performing art events happening as well as numerous ways to get creative — from art courses to craft workshops.
explore the arts















research and innovation
as a billion-dollar research enterprise situated in the heart of one of the most densely concentrated innovation hubs in the nation, uc san diego is a unique place where fresh ideas are translated into solutions to benefit society—from climate science and the human microbiome to nanotechnology and social mobility.


more about research








preparing for the next pandemic
the centers for disease control and prevention (cdc) has selected uc san diego as one of three institutions to lead the charm network, a major initiative aimed at improving pandemic preparedness. as part of the five-year cooperative agreement, school of medicine researchers will study the spread of respiratory viruses and immune responses across diverse populations, serving as a central data hub for the nationwide effort.
