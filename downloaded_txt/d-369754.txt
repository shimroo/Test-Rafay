an inclusive & accessible society is possible.














working together to eliminate barriers to full and equal participation



manitoba possible (formerly society for manitobans with disabilities) provides a wide range of services to manitobans with disabilities, their families, and their communities. through collaboration, diversity and innovation, we are creating a more inclusive and accessible society for all.

join us for asl immersion, organized by manitoba possible’s deaf and hard of hearing program. this week-long experience is open to everyone in the community. taught by deaf instructors mix of classrooms and workshops— opportunity to learn asl and about deaf culture in a fun and supportive way. whether it’s your first time learning signed or to advanced learners.

faqs








            how do we learn?
          









asl classes run from 9:00 am to 3:15 pm, with short coffee breaks and a one-hour lunch. different teachers through the classes, focusing on various aspects of asl.workshops run monday to thursday from 3:30 to 4:30 pm. topics include deaf culture, fingerspelling, and more. you can join the session that interests you or matches your asl level.








            can youth attend?
          









mature youth 13 yrs of age and older may attend. participants under 16 years of age must be accompanied by an adult who is also attending asl immersion. youth who have aides or workers in their regular school programs will also need an aide/worker to attend the immersion classes (free for aide/worker).








            are there tests?
          









no tests—everyone passes!the program is a place to learn asl and get to know deaf people and their culture in a fun and relaxed way.












            what is the cost?
          









program cost: asl immersion – $300optional lunch program: +$60fun & sign summer camp: $135








            is lunch included?
          









you can choose to opt in to the lunch program for additional fee of $60. lunches are catered by camp kakepitay (deaf camp), with all proceeds supporting the camp. you can download the menu and more information here (link)on wednesday, we provide free pizza for all students, staff & children attending. this lunch is provided regardless of registering for the lunch program.coffee, tea and juice is available for all participants throughout the week free of charge.if you are bringing your own lunch, please note it is a peanut and nut free building.








            what if i have to withdraw?
          









you have until june 14 to receive a full refund less a $50 administration fee.students withdrawing after june 14 will not receive a refund.
