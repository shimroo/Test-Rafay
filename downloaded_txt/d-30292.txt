...flood warning now in effect until 10 am est monday...

* what...urban area and small stream flooding caused by excessive
rainfall continue.

* where...portions of east central, northeast, south central, and
southeast kentucky, including the following counties, in east
central kentucky, estill. in northeast kentucky, johnson and
martin. in south central kentucky, laurel and rockcastle. in
southeast kentucky, breathitt, clay, floyd, harlan, jackson,
knott, lee, leslie, letcher, magoffin, owsley, perry, pike and
wolfe.

* when...until 1000 am est monday.

* impacts...flooding of rivers, creeks, streams, and other low-lying
and flood-prone locations is imminent or occurring. numerous roads
remain closed due to flooding.

* additional details...
- at 445 pm est, the public, law enforcement, and emergency
management reported flooding still occurring from heavy rain
in the warned area. between 3 and 6 inches of rain fell on
saturday.
- some locations that will experience flooding include...
pikeville, hazard, prestonsburg, mount vernon, jackson,
whitesburg, salyersville, beattyville, mckee, hindman,
campton, hyden, booneville, boone, letcher, mason, simpson,
estill, bath and cumberland.


precautionary/preparedness actions...

turn around, don't drown when encountering flooded roads. most flood
deaths occur in vehicles.

please report observed flooding to local emergency services or law
enforcement and request they pass this information to the national
weather service when you can do so safely.

weather alert


...winter weather advisory in effect until 4 am est monday...

* what...snow showers expected. total snow accumulations generally
up to 1 inch, with isolated amounts up to 2 inches. additionally,
lingering moisture on untreated surfaces will likely freeze and
lead to black ice as temperatures drop into the upper teens to
lower twenties overnight.

* where...portions of east central, northeast, south central, and
southeast kentucky.

* when...until 4 am est monday.

* impacts...plan on slippery road conditions.

* additional details...the snow could be briefly heavy and lead to
rapid deterioration in travel conditions. impacts will be variable
over short distances due to the showery nature of the snowfall.

precautionary/preparedness actions...

slow down and use caution while traveling. the latest road
conditions for kentucky can be found by visiting https://goky.ky.gov.
