lirmm 












le laboratoire d’informatique, de robotique et de microélectronique de montpellier – lirmm – est une unité mixte de recherche, dépendant conjointement de l’université montpellier (um) et du centre national de la recherche scientifique. 











le lirmm a pour tutelles secondaires l’université paul valéry montpellier (upvm), l’université perpignan via domitia (upvd) et l’institut national de recherche en sciences et technologies du numérique (inria). il est situé sur le campus saint-priest de l’um. ses activités de recherche positionnent pleinement le lirmm au coeur des sciences et technologies de l’information, de la communication et des systèmes. les travaux sont menés dans trois départements scientifiques de recherche, eux-mêmes organisés en équipes…suite

imran meghazi, «improved indirect encoding to solve travelling salesman problem with variational quantum algorithms»the travelling salesman problem (tsp) is an emblematic problem from operations research. its objective is to find the shortest path going through a set of city exactly once and coming…the travelling salesman problem (tsp) is an emblematic problem from operations research.
its objective is to find the shortest path going through a set of city exactly once and coming
back to the starting point. in the last 10 years, several quantum computer architectures have
emerged. this raised the following question : how well does this new computing paradigm
perform on operations research problems and especially on tsp ?
variational quantum algorithms are amongst the most promising quantum algorithms for
or. they combine classical and quantum strengths. however, due to the current hardware
limitations (number of qubits, noise during computation), instances solved are trivial. these
kinds of limitations motivates to find alternative ways to model well-known problems, such as
for example indirect encoding.
this presentation focuses on a similar encoding, although this one tries to preserve distance between
solutions, which is very important for gradient-based algorithms.https://info-web.lirmm.fr/collorg/6376fe23-9ec1-4951-ac0d-ba0d1a18799a 15 h 15 min													– 16 h 15 min

haetham al aswad, «computing discrete logarithms in finite fields faster with galois automorphisms»the number field sieve (nfs) algorithm and its variants are the best algorithms to solve the discrete logarithm problem in finite fields. we will first take a look on how…the number field sieve (nfs) algorithm and its variants are the best algorithms to solve the discrete logarithm problem in finite fields.
we will first take a look on how nfs works, and second, we will explore how galois automorphisms can accelerate the hardest steps of nfs by quite large factors.
we will discuss an open problem of using galois automorphisms of any order, and present our work that solves the problem for the two orders 6 and 12—whereas the previous solved orders stand at the only order 2.
consequently, this brings acceleration factors approximately equal to 36 and 144 to one of the two hardest steps in nfs, surpassing the prior record acceleration factor of 4.
prior knowledge of nfs is not required.https://info-web.lirmm.fr/collorg/696a927b-1288-47f0-bdaf-6fa0d24faf14
