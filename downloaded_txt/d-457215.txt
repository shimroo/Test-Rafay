located 17 miles east of santa margarita, california in the once forgotten frontier town of pozo, the historic pozo saloon has welcomed weary travelers since 1858.


























surviving prohibition and more recently a pandemic, the saloon has stood the test of time--with its original redwood old-west facade and hitching posts remaining beneath the very same cottonwood tree that was planted at its founding over a century ago. 


























today, the saloon enters a new era as it joins nomada hotel group, who are working to preserve the historic landmark and honor its legacy for decades to come.
