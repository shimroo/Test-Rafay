a comprehensive guide to asbestos removal on the gold coast 
posted on october 2, 2023 by charlene filipi 


 

asbestos removal on the gold coast is a very important process for keeping this popular tourist destination, and its many attractions, safe and healthy. in recent years, completion of the queensland government’s state-wide program to remove asbestos-contaminated properties has made certain areas on the gold cowebsite ecially attractive locations to visit.


understanding the dangers of asbestos
inhalation of asbestos fibers can lead to serious health risks such as mesothelioma, asbestosis, and lung cancer. exposure to even small amounts of airborne asbestos can have a devastating effect on the respiratory systems of those affected. it is therefore essential that any buildings or structures built before the mid-1980s must be assessed for potential asbestos contamination before any refurbishment work takes place.
how asbestos removal is carried out on the gold coast
the gold coast city council provides a comprehensive guide on how to identify and manage asbestos on their website. they recommend hiring a licensed expert to carry out asbestos testing in order to ensure the safety of people living or working within the vicinity. such experts are highly trained and qualified to identify and conduct efficient asbestos removal services on the gold coast.
asbestos removal teams follow a strict set of guidelines which include notifying neighbours of the works taking place, covering nearby premises, measuring the air quality after and during the removal process, and ensuring workers wear protective clothing. the ultimate goal of such measures is to minimize the potential exposure to asbestos fibers by all parties.
how to identify possible asbestos contamination
due to its robust properties, asbestos was widely used in building construction prior to its ban in 1989. home owners should look for common signs of asbestos in order to identify any potential risk. common items with possible asbestos content include roof sheets, soffit boards, vinyl flooring, ceiling tiles, lagging materials, insulation boards and sealants. should there be a suspicion of asbestos contamination, it is highly recommended that further inspection and testing be done by a professional.
what to do if asbestos is present?
