san gabriel nursery & florist is one of southern california's largest and most complete garden centers. we have been family-owned and operated since the nursery was founded in 1923. our many years of experience include extensive knowledge about the plants, climate and growing conditions conducive to southern california.
























we are honored to have been serving southern california for over 100 years!founded in 1923, san gabriel nursery & florist is a family-owned and operated business, specializing in the rare and hard to find. we have a two-acre retail nursery featuring a complete garden supply center and full service florist. three generations of family members along with an extensive staff, continue the tradition of providing quality nursery stock at reasonable prices.

we offer local delivery service. we deliver monday to saturday and can arrange for either am or pm deliveries. our delivery rates vary within the different delivery zones. each delivery zone has its own minimum purchase requirement and delivery fee based on your particular delivery zone.  please inquire about alternative delivery service companies that customers may hire directly for areas outside of our local delivery zones.  note:   please be advised that due to insurance restrictions, we will only deliver merchandise to the curb or a reasonable unloading area at the nearest point of entrance to your property. sgn&f drivers will unload merchandise only within 10 feet from our vehicle per driver’s discretion. we are not able to deliver up driveways, steep walkways, hillsides or to any location on the property that cannot be easily accessed. in addition, our driver is not able to spot place plants.  depending on necessity and size of your order, please make your own arrangements to have assistance at the time of delivery.

cdfa working to save california’s citrus!  the asian citrus psyllid (acp) is a pest that acts as a carrier or vector spreading huanglongbing (hlb), a devastating disease of citrus trees. this bacterial disease is transmitted to healthy trees by the psyllid after it feeds on infected plant tissue. as we are now in one of the asian citrus psyllid/huanglongbing disease quarantine zones, we are not able to sell any citrus trees until further notice. for more information on the regulation and quarantine boundaries, please click on the following california department of food & agriculture link https://www.cdfa.ca.gov/citrus/pests_diseases/acp/regulation.html
